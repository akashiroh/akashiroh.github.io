Welcome to my website!

I'm Andrew Holmes, a machine learning researcher, data scientist, and AI engineer specializing in application-driven machine learning. I am currently a graduate research assistant and teaching assistant at Western Washington University finishing up my Master's in Computer Science.

My main focus of research has been in developing deep learning-based climate emulators, decreasing the compute necessary to explore spatial and temporal dynamics between climate and economic sectors. I also have experience in natural language processing, deploying and finetuning state-of-the-art models, and computer vision.

Projects

Grapheme to Phoneme Machine Translation
CSCI 481 — Deep Learning
This project was part of a Deep Learning course at Western Washington University. I trained a bi-directional GRU model on English grapheme-phoneme pairs to better encode pronunciation patterns, achieving a 9.89% word (phoneme) error rate — second-best in a class of 35+ students.
report
GCAMnet
Collaboration with the Joint Global Change Research Institute and Pacific Northwest National Lab
We explore GCAM ensemble designs by training a neural network to emulate two case studies (renewable energy, food scarcity), reducing required simulation data by 50%. The emulator efficiently discovers optimal input configurations for desirable climate futures.
GitHub arXiv
Mayapán Structure Semantic Segmentation
Collaboration with the Archaeology Department
Fine-tuned a semantic segmentation model on aerial lidar data for binary pixel classification. Training on 50m × 50m patches (elevation, returns, intensity, relative elevation) achieved 0.56 F1 and 0.41 mIoU — in line with other work in this domain.
