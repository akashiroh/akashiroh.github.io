Welcome to my website!

I'm Andrew Holmes, a {machine learning researcher, data scientist, and ai engineer} specializing in application-driven machine learning. I am currently a graduate research assistant and teaching assistant at Western Washington University finishing up my Master's in Computer Science.

My main focus of research has been in developing deep learning-based climate emulators, decreasing the compute necessary to explore spatial and temporal dynamics between climate and economic sectors.

I enjoy developing simple solutions to complex problems and have worked closely with domain experts to train, fine-tune, and deploy state-of-the-art or developed models in many interdisciplinary fields. I have experience working in climate science, natural and spoken lange processing, animal welfare research, archaeology, bioinformatics, and computer vision. Each field has taught me something new and useful that has allowed me to adapt easily to new fields.

Projects

Grapheme to Phoneme Machine Translation
CSCI 481 — Deep Learning
Trained a bi-directional GRU model on English grapheme-phoneme pairs to better encode pronunciation patterns, achieving a 9.89% word (phoneme) error rate; second-best performance in a class of 35+ students.
report
GCAMnet
Collaboration with the Joint Global Change Research Institute and Pacific Northwest National Lab
We explore GCAM ensemble designs by training a neural network to emulate two case studies {renewable energy, food scarcity}, reducing required simulation data by 50%. This emulator can be used to (1) identify regions in the input space of high uncertainty to efficiently sample a training dataset and (2) discover an optimal set of inputs that maximizes/minimizes select outputs.
GitHub arXiv
Mayapán Structure Semantic Segmentation
Collaboration with the Archaeology Department
Fine-tuned a semantic segmentation model on aerial lidar data for binary pixel classification. Training on 50m × 50m patches with 11 channels {elevation, returns, intensity, varying relative elevation} achieved 0.56 F1 and 0.41 mIoU. Our targets are ground surveyed instead of lidar-derived, making this task harder than other works in this field.
